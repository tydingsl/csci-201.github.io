Pig Latin

(Adapted from Introduction to Programming with Java: A Problem-Solving Approach, Dean and Dean, McGraw-Hill.)

 

Write a program that converts words to pig Latin. Words that begin with a vowel are unchanged. Words that begin with a consonant have the starting consonant moved to the end of the word and “ay” is appended after the consonant. If the input is only the single letter “q” or “Q,” the program quits. Given the inputs “open,” “door,” and “q,” make the program produce the following display:

 

Sample session:

 

PIG LATIN WORD CONVERTER

 

Enter a word: open

In Pig Latin, that's "open"

Enter a word: door

In Pig Latin, that's "oorday"

Enter a word: q

I hope you enjoyed your porcine experience! Please come again.
